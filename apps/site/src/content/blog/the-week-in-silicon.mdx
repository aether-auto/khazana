Three things happened this week that look unrelated until you line them up.

First, a research paper argued that the routing inside <Annotation client:load term="mixture-of-experts" note="A model whose layers route each token to a few specialized sub-networks ('experts'), so only a fraction of the weights run per token." /> models is doing something subtler than load balancing — it's reproducing a *curriculum*.<Sidenote n={1} href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01234" source="Sparse MoE paper">The paper measures expert-assignment entropy falling monotonically through training — the network teaches itself an easy-to-hard ordering nobody specified.</Sidenote>

The marginal cost of inference has been falling on both tiers, but at very different rates. Watch the edge curve draw itself in:

The edge curve crossed the datacenter curve this spring. But *when* it crosses
depends entirely on one number — the decay rate. Take the model's controls and
find the crossover yourself: drag the rate and watch the half-life and the month
it drops under a dollar move with you.
