This is a QA harness for the **P0 wave**. Each of the five new components appears below with realistic props and then deliberately stressed with long text, many rows, and narrow-viewport edge cases. The running prose is intentionally verbose so inline components like <Definition client:visible term="Definition" def="a glossary tooltip that teaches a term, distinguished from Annotation by a dotted amber underline — hover, tap, or focus to reveal the meaning." /> have real text to attach to, and so wrapping and reflow can be judged against a genuine paragraph rather than a single short line that hides bugs.

Block math also renders inline: mass–energy is $E = mc^2$, and a display equation set with `$$` fences renders at build time with zero runtime JS:

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2}\,dx = \sqrt{\pi} $$

## 5. Definition — inline, rich children, mid-sentence

The term <Definition client:visible term="entropy" def="In information theory, the average number of bits needed to encode outcomes drawn from a distribution — a measure of uncertainty." /> is distinct from a citation: it teaches rather than sources.

A definition can also carry richer children in place of the plain string: <Definition client:visible term="children"><>Rich <strong>JSX children</strong> render inside the popover instead of the plain <code>def</code> string.</></Definition>.

Several definitions in one paragraph — <Definition client:visible term="measure" def="A 65-character prose column, the sacred reading width." />, <Definition client:visible term="bleed" def="How far a figure may widen past the prose column: column, wide, or full." />, and <Definition client:visible term="hairline" def="A one-pixel rule — the house grammar is lines, not boxes." /> — confirm that multiple mid-line popovers coexist without widening the page at 360px.
