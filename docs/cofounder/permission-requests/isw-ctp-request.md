## Subject

Written permission request — incorporating ISW/CTP control geometry into a non-commercial static Atlas

## Body

Hello ISW / Critical Threats Project team,

I am writing to request **written permission** under your Fair Use and Attribution Policy. I
understand that policy requires **prior written permission** to incorporate ISW/CTP materials
into **other datasets, mapping platforms, analytic products, or systems**, and that it bars
redistribution in bulk, via API, or through automated means. This message asks for exactly that
permission and does **not** assert any current right — today khazana only **links out** to
ISW/CTP and uses independent fallback geometry.

**About the project:** khazana is a personal, $0-cost, static/serverless "Atlas" surface. It is
**private today and public-ready** — it may become publicly viewable later, and I want any use of
your materials explicitly authorized in writing before that happens.

I would like written confirmation of whether you can authorize — and on what terms — each of:

1. **Automated retrieval** of ISW/CTP control geometry into an automated pipeline.
2. **Private retention / storage** of retrieved geometry in a private repository.
3. **Transformation / rendering / embedding** as a re-styled map layer (client-visible), or, if
   that is not permitted, a **static rendered derivative** instead.
4. **Attribution requirements** — the exact credit line, logo, and link-back you require.
5. **Update cadence / refresh limits** for automated retrieval.
6. **Restrictions on client-visible map data.**
7. **Retention & revocation** terms.
8. **Private vs. future-public use** — whether a future **public** rendering would require a
   **separate** approval.

I also acknowledge that your policy covers incorporation into datasets, mapping platforms,
analytic products, or systems, and I would like to confirm **whether any additional rights-holder
approval is required** (for example, where CTP is a joint AEI project).

To be explicit about what khazana will **not** do: it will **not** proxy or re-expose your API or
data, will **not** operate a raw-data download browser, will **not** resell your materials, and
will **not** represent ISW/CTP assessments as its own. If you cannot authorize embedded /
client-visible geometry, please tell me whether a **static rendered derivative** is permitted;
otherwise I will keep khazana's current **link-out-only** posture.

Please also point me to the correct contact if press@understandingwar.org is not the right route
for this request. Thank you for your work and for considering this.

— khazana (founder)
